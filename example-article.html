Bad training vs. better training

Early on, I chased random exercises, crushed myself with volume, and expected my body to magically adapt. Over time, I realized that what matters most is a mix of:

  • Practicing the actual skill—jumping.
  • Strength work that makes sense for that skill.
  • Enough rest that you can show up and actually jump high.

What this means for you as an athlete

If you’re one of my athletes, your plan is built around those same ideas. We don’t chase soreness for the sake of it. We chase progress: higher jumps, faster approaches, and better consistency.

When you read your training plan, keep that in mind. It’s not about filling every possible day with work—it’s about stacking the right kind of work over and over.
