Sample Vertical Jump Block (Weeks 1–3)

Example plan • This is just to show structure. Real plans are individualized.

This sample block is written as if you’re training 3–4 days per week with a focus on dunking or jumping higher. Volume and intensity would be adjusted based on you as an athlete.

Week 1

Day 1 – Jump session + lower body

  • Approach jumps: 4 × 3 jumps (full rest between sets)
  • Squats: 3 × 5 (moderate-heavy)
  • Romanian deadlifts: 3 × 6–8
  • Core work: 3 sets

Day 2 – Light plyos + upper body

  • Pogo jumps: 3 × 10–15
  • Box push-ups: 3 × 8–12
  • Row variation: 3 × 8–12

Day 3 – Jump session + lighter lower body

  • Approach jumps: 5 × 2 (focus on quality)
  • Split squats: 3 × 6–8 each leg
  • Calf raises: 3 × 12–15

Week 2

Very similar structure to Week 1, with small adjustments in volume or load based on how you responded.

  • Day 1: Slight increase in squat load if last week felt solid.
  • Day 2: Keep volume similar; focus on clean landings.
  • Day 3: Same or slightly fewer jumps if you’re feeling beat up.

Week 3

Either a small step forward from Week 2 or a slight deload if you’ve had a lot of heavy life/training stress. The exact choice depends on the athlete.

  • Day 1: Quality jumps + 2–3 sets instead of 3–4 heavy sets.
  • Day 2: Keep it light and snappy.
  • Day 3: Slightly lower volume and focus on feeling bouncy.

For real athletes, this page would include more specific sets, loads, and notes along with adjustments based on your check-ins.
